# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a form of cybercrime in which perpetrators pose as reliable sources in order to fool victims into divulging private information, such as passwords, bank account information, or OTPs. This typically occurs through phony emails, messages, or websites that appear authentic.


For instance, a college student might receive an email purporting to be from a bank informing them that their account will be blocked unless they promptly confirm their information. A link in the email looks authentic, but it actually takes users to a phony website that mimics the bank's official website.


After clicking the link, the student inputs their OTP and login information. After the data is submitted, the attacker can access the student's bank account and carry out illegal transactions.

Students and novice internet users who might not be able to recognize phony messages are the primary targets of phishing attacks. Financial loss, identity theft, and improper use of personal data are among the repercussions.

Users should always confirm the source of emails, refrain from clicking on dubious links, and never divulge private information online without adequate verification in order to prevent such attacks.
